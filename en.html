Dear friends,


I am honored to invite you to participate in the 4th RETINA LVIV 2023 Symposium, "Vitreoretinal Surgery and More", on July 7-8, 2023 in Lviv, Ukraine.

We decided to extend the format of our symposium to make it more appealing to a wider circle of ophthalmologists. Apart from vitreoretinal surgery, we will be discussing relevant topics in various areas of ophthalmology.

As usual, opportunities will be created for broad discussions between panelists and the audience.

Despite the aggressive war russia is waging against Ukraine, we have to display our resilience, not only on the battlefield, but in every domain of our activities. I believe our symposium will be yet another small step to our victory.

All the funds collected at the symposium will be donated to the armed forces of Ukraine.

Glory to Ukraine!


Ihor Novytsky

ABSTRACT SUBMISSION


Abstract submission deadline: June 4, 2023, midnight.

Video submission deadline: June 11, 2023, midnight.


REQUIREMENTS

  • All abstracts are to be submitted via email to prof.novytskyy@gmail.com.
  • Abstracts sent via regular mail, fax or otherwise will NOT be accepted.
  • Upon receipt of your abstract, you will receive a brief email. Please check your email to make sure all information is correct. Organizers assume no responsibility for abstracts submitted not in line with the above requirements.
  • All abstracts will be reviewed, assessed and accepted or declined by the organizing committee.
  • The organizing committee reserves the right to select abstracts to be presented. All decisions of the organizing committee will be final.
  • If an abstract is accepted, the presented needs to register for the symposium.


STRUCTURE

All abstracts must be written in English or in Ukrainian according to the following template:

  • title, introduction, objective, methods, results, conclusions, financial disclosures from all authors.
  • Abstracts may not exceed 1 page.
  • All abbreviations should be expanded when used for the first time.


COMPLEX CASE PRESENTATIONS

  • Only one case per presentation is permitted.
  • A presentation must not exceed 5 slides.
  • 3 minutes are allocated for each presentation.


NUMBER OF ABSTRACTS

  • Each author may submit the following:
    • maximum of 3 reports (2 for the vitreoretinal session and 1 for other sessions, or 1 for the vitreoretinal session, and 1 for each of the other sessions);
    • maximum of 1 video clip;
    • maximum of 1 complex case.


Allocated time

  • Presentation: 7 minutes
  • Video clips: up to 5 minutes
  • Difficult case presentation: 3 minutes
  • Presentation by foreign colleagues, with simultaneous interpretation: 10-15 minutes.
